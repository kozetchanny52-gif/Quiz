History

St.Esprit High School Entry-way
St Esprit High School, located in Gihosha, Bujumbura, Burundi, has a rich and complex history dating back to 1952. The first Jesuits arrived in Rwanda at the request of Mwami Mutara III Rudahigwa, King of Rwanda, and opened a secondary school in Kabgayi, which quickly became a center of academic excellence.
Today, Lycée du Saint-Esprit is a co-educational public secondary school, located in Gihosha, with around 920 students. Teaching is in French, with Kirundi, Kiswahili, and English also taught. The school is considered one of the best in the country, with outstanding national exam results . Academic Excellence





St Esprit High School is known for its academic excellence, with a strong curriculum that includes rigorous programs in sciences and languages, preparing students for national exams and future studies. Experienced faculty provide high quality instruction and guidance, helping students achieve excellent results, with outstanding national exam performance and top university placements. There's also a strong emphasis on critical thinking, encouraging problem solving and intellectual curiosity. 😊

Holistic Education




St Esprit High school offers a diverse and vibrant range of extracurricular activities, including language clubs, debate teams, sports teams, music ensembles, art clubs, and cultural events, carefully designed to foster student development, creativity, teamwork skills, and social responsibility, while promoting cultural exchange and understanding among students of different backgrounds and interests, and providing opportunities for students to discover new passions, develop leadership skills, and create lasting memories with peers .

Mission

At St Esprit High school, our mission is to inspire and empower students to become compassionate, creative, and critical thinkers, equipped with the knowledge, skills, and values to succeed in an ever-changing world and to make an impact on communities.
